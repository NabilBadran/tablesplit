# TableSplit

**Scan. Split. Pay.**

TableSplit is a web app that lets a table of diners split and pay their restaurant bill straight from their own phones — no app to download, no account to create, no card passed around the table.

---

## The problem

Splitting a bill at a restaurant is almost always awkward. One person fronts the whole thing and chases everyone later, the table does mental maths over who had the extra glass of wine, or a server waits while a single card gets passed around. TableSplit removes all of that.

## How it works

**For diners**

1. Scan the QR code on your table.
2. The bill appears live on your phone.
3. Tap the items you personally ordered.
4. Choose a service charge and pay your share.

That's it. Shared a bottle of grape juice? Split that dish a few ways with a tap. Want to keep it simple? The whole table can split everything equally in one tap. Each person only ever pays for what they had, and their service charge applies to their own total — so no one's stuck covering someone else's order.

Everything updates in real time. The moment one person claims an item, it disappears from everyone else's available list, so two people can't accidentally pay for the same dish.

**For restaurant staff**

Staff use a simple dashboard — a floor plan of the tables. They tap a table to add what was ordered, watch payments come in live, and reset the table when guests leave. No training required.

## What makes it nice to use

- **Nothing to install** — it all runs in the phone's browser from a QR scan.
- **No logins** — diners stay anonymous; they just pay and go.
- **Fair by design** — split shared items, split the whole bill equally, or pay only for your own.
- **Live and in sync** — every phone at the table sees the same up-to-date bill.
- **Emailed receipts** — diners can get a digital receipt sent to them after paying.

## About this version

This is a **working demo**, themed around a fictional modern-Italian restaurant called *Caffè Lumo*. Payments are simulated — no real money changes hands and no card details are ever entered — so you can try the full experience end to end safely.

It's built with Next.js and Supabase, and hosted on Vercel.
